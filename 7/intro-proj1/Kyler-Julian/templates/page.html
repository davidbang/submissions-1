Crime in the United States: 1990-2009

Are We Moving in the Right Direction?

Looking at crime rates and number of occurences over the years, one can see the change over time. With the data gathered from the FBI's public database, we have gathered the information for crime from 1990 to 2009 in order to see if we as a country have dropped our crime rates over twenty years or if we have raised them. Are we becoming better people or worse people? Are those in charge of law enforcement doing their duties? Lets find out.

Conclusion

Between 1990 and 2009, the total acounts of crime dropped from 28951226 to 21278738, a difference of nearly eigth million. Some crimes by themselves saw a difference in number of accounts between their high point and low point to be as high as over 3.5 million, and the difference in rate to be as high as over 2,000. According to this data, we can draw the conclusion that perhaps the United States is, in fact, becoming a safer place as far as crime. Maybe the people are holding higher moral standards, or law enforcement is doing their duty more efficiently. Hopefully these numbers will continue to decrease as the years progress.
