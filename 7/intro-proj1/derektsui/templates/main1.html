Technical Terms

This analysis focuses on technical analysis of stock data, mainly used to analyze past price trends in predicting future movements.

Change -- The change in price from the previous day.

Moving Average (MA) -- an average of the previous few days of a stock's performance. The MA(10), for example, averages the last 10 days of data. When a smaller number of days is used, it reflects a short-term average.
     When a short-term average crosses above a long-term average it is generally seen as a bullish (positive) sign, because recent performance is improving relative to past performance.

Relative Strength Index (RSI) -- a measure, btw. 0 and 100, of the relative strength of a stock within previous few days. Reflects magnitude of ups vs. downs.
     The higher the RSI, the stronger its recent performance. However, RSIs of 30 and 70 are seen as oversold and overbought, respectively, and are typical reversal points.
